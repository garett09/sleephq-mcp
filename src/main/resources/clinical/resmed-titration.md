# ResMed titration rules (Therapy Handbook 10114280r1, condensed)

## Pressure adjustment triggers

Increase pressure by **at least 1 cmH2O** when any of these recur in a single night:
- ≥ 2 obstructive apneas (OAs)
- ≥ 3 hypopneas
- ≥ 5 respiratory effort–related arousals (RERAs)
- ≥ 3 minutes of snoring

## Central apnea / TECSA management

- If central apneas rise: **decrease 1 cmH2O**, wait 20 minutes, reassess
- If central apneas persist after pressure reduction → **TECSA suspected** → consider ASV evaluation
- Do **not** enable EPR (Expiratory Pressure Relief) Full Time — established TECSA risk factor
- Current EPR setting is Off; keep it that way

## Leak threshold

- ResMed spec: large leak flagged at **> 24 L/min**
- Persistent leak above threshold invalidates the night's stats; recheck mask fit before adjusting pressure

## Escalation criteria

Escalate care (in-person sleep medicine review) only if:
- SpO2 regularly below 85%
- AHI consistently > 20 / hr despite titration
- New cardiac symptoms or daytime concerns
